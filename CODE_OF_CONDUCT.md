# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to making participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Thanking contributors for their efforts
- Being patient with new contributors

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Discrimination based on the characteristics listed above
- Threatening violence or violent language directed against another person
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following (online or in person)
- Sustained disruption of community discussions or events

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community. Examples of representing a project or community include using an official project e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event. Representation of a project may be further defined and clarified by project maintainers.

## Conflict Resolution

### For Community Members

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct:

1. **Document the incident**: Save relevant messages, screenshots, or details
2. **Report to maintainers**: Email conduct@example.com with details
3. **Respect privacy**: We will keep your report confidential

### For Maintainers

When enforcing this Code of Conduct:

1. **Acknowledge** the report and confirm receipt
2. **Investigate** thoroughly and fairly
3. **Communicate** with the involved party
4. **Decide** on appropriate action
5. **Document** the decision and action taken

## Reporting Violations

To report a violation, please email: **conduct@example.com**

Include in your report:
- Description of the incident
- Location/channel where it occurred (GitHub issue, PR, discussion, etc.)
- When it occurred
- Who was involved
- How it affected you or others
- Any evidence (links, screenshots, messages)
- What action you'd like taken (if any)

**All reports will be treated confidentially.** We are committed to maintaining the safety and well-being of our community.

## Enforcement Examples

Maintainers will apply the following guidelines when determining consequences for Code of Conduct violations:

### 1. Correction

**Severity**: Minor violation, first incident

**Action**: Private written warning, explaining the issue and asking for correction

**Timeline**: Immediate, with 24 hours to respond

### 2. Warning

**Severity**: Moderate violation or repeated minor violations

**Action**: 
- Public warning on the reported issue/PR
- Temporary restrictions on participation
- Required attendance to discussion about the violation

**Timeline**: 1-7 days depending on severity

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Severity**: Serious violation or repeated warnings

**Action**:
- Temporary ban from contributing (1-4 weeks)
- Restrictions on community participation
- Required reflection period before reinstatement

**Timeline**: Implemented immediately, review after period

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Severity**: Severe/repeated violations or threatening behavior

**Action**:
- Permanent removal from project
- All contributions may be reverted or locked
- Community safety prioritized

**Timeline**: Immediate and final
